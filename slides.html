Glacial Climate Variability

Literature Review

Matt Griffiths

Experimental data

  • In 2012 the PALAEOSENS project team published a paper in Nature

  • They outlined new findings linking the variability of the Earth's temperature to global glaciation periods

  • They failed to produce a working theory which explained these fluctuations
PALAEOSENS, Making sense of palaeoclimate sensitivity, Nature, 2012, doi 10.1038, issue 11574

Determining the cause

  • In 2014 Von der Heydt proposed a variety of climate subsystems which operate on the appropriate timescale to be linked to the PALAEOSENS data.

  • In 2015 Köhler determined that the physical phenomena responsible for climate variability was most likely combination of a temperature dependent albedo and light scattering in the atmosphere.

This leaves us with:

  • Multiple anomalies in experimentally recorded archeological climate data

  • A series of specific physical phenomena which could increase climate variance

  • A list of climate subsystems which could modulate the driving mechanisms

The gap in the theory:

However there is no currently accepted physical model relating the climate subsystems to the driving mechanisms directly.

This leaves a hole in the current understanding of glacial period climate behavior
